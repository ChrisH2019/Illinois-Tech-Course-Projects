  • Taylor Street

    © Elizabeth Atkinson/timeout.com

    Home to Chicago’s Little Italy, Taylor street houses some of the best Italian Restaurants and Bakeries in the city. A number of buses run along or across Taylor Street, providing easy transportation. (Walk Score 91)

  • Fulton Market

    © fultonmarketkitchen.com

    Granted Landmark District status in 2015, Fulton Market has replaced the meat-packing plants and warehouses of its distant past with hotels, bars, restaurants, and stores, turning it into one of Chicago’s top commercial districts. Morgan Station on the Green and Pink lines, as well as a number of bus lines, provide easy transportation.

    Learn More >>

  • Greektown

    © greektownchicago.org

    Home to both varied restaurants and stores as well as a cultural museum and an annual parade, Greektown has managed to cultivate itself as a nightlife and retail district while remaining essentially Greek, making it an excellent place to visit.

    Learn More >>

  • Chinatown

    © Pulaw/Flickr

    Just North of Armour Square, Chinatown is a vibrant district still growing in population. Aside from the huge variety of restaurants and stores it houses, Chinatown also includes worthwhile sights like Chinatown Gate and the Nine Dragon Wall, a replica of a mural outside the Forbidden City in Beijing. (Walk Score 96)

    Learn More >>

  • River North

    © wikipedia.org

    A vibrant and growing neighborhood within the Near North Side, River North houses famous locations like the House of Blues, Trump Tower, and the Wrigley Building. River North is best-known for its nightlife, fine dining, and river walk. (Walk Score 96)

    © brooklynboulders.com/

    First Ascent

    © asweatlife.com

    The most wide spread climbing gym in Chicago, First ascent offers the following 3 locations listed below.

    1. Uptown - bouldering only gym. (Still offers workout equipment and yoga classes). Just south of the Lawrence Red Line stop, on broadway. (Walk Score 91)

    2. Avondale -the original First Ascent location. Offers bouldering, top rope climbing, sport climbing, workout equipment yoga. 10 minute walk east of the Addison blue line stop. (Walk Score 84)

    3. Block 37 - The closest gym to IIT Campus. This is a bouldering only gym. (Still offers workout equipment and yoga classes). Just north of the Washington/Wabash Green Line stop on State St. (Walk Score 93)

  • Beaches

    Oak Street Beach

    © travoh.com

    One of the smaller beaches. This is located in the Gold Coast area and has many nice shops and restaurants on the way to and from. Take the redline to Clark/Division and walk east to the lake and it will be the first beach south. (Walk Score 96)

    Learn More >>

    North Avenue Beach

    © choosechicago.com

    One of the more popular beaches and definitely the most crowded. North avenue draws people from all walks of life between the beach volleyball courts and Castaway’s (The beach restaurant/bar). Simply take the red line to Clark/Division and walk east to the lake and then north past Oak Street beach. (Walk Score 96)

    Learn More >>

    Montrose Avenue Beach

    © marinas.com

    The most north of the public beaches and also the largest. Montrose beach offers expansive grass fields for sports and BBQs alike. This is also the only beach with a section where dogs are allowed. To get here, you must ride the red line all the way up to the Montrose stop and walk east to the beach. (Walk Score 79)

    Learn More >>

    Margaret T. Burroughs Beach

    © Performance Impressions LLC

    A straight shot down 31st street. This beach is a little less popular but still offers BBQ pits and plenty of open space to explore. There is also a skatepark for those into skating or BMX. (Walk Score 72)

    Learn More >>

  • Lakefront Trail

    © architecture.org

    A very popular trail for runners and cyclists alike. It is an 18.5 mile long trail that spans from South Shore all the way up to Edgewater. The easiest way to get there from campus would be to walk straight east down 31st street. (Walk Score 85)

    Learn More >>

  • Garfield Park Conservatory

    © garfieldconservatory.org

    Huge green house with various themed rooms based on climate. Contains an extremely wide variety of plant species. Also has a beehive that they use to teach people about honey bees. The Conservatory is free, but donations are appreciated. To get there, take the Green Line to Conservatory-Central Park Drive. (Walk Score 78)

  • Cloud Gate (Bean)

    © inspiredimperfection.com

    Officially known as Cloud Gate, the Bean is an enormous Stainless Steel sculpture located in Millenium Park. It’s known more familiarly as the Bean because of its shape, and stands as one of Chicago’s best-known landmarks. (Walk Score 98)

    Learn More >>

  • Navy Pier

    © hotelpalomar-chicago.com

    With more than fifty acres of parks, shops, restaurants, and other attractions, Navy Pier has more than 2 million visitors every year. The 3,300 foot long pier stands as Chicago’s number one tourist attraction and one of the top destinations in all the midwest. (Walk Score 95)

    Learn More >>

  • Willis Tower

    © wikipedia.org

    The Willis Tower, formerly and best known as the Sears Tower, is the second tallest building in the United States and in the Western Hemisphere. Its observation deck, from which you can look down upon the city, draws over a million visitors every year. (Walk Score 97)

    Learn More >>

  • Museum of Science and Industry

    © choosechicago.com

    Chicago’s best known museum, it houses a German Uboat captured during World War II, the Command Model of Apollo 8, a full-size replica coal mine, and a great number of other, equally interesting exhibits. Located in Jackson Park, the museum has operated as a non-profit since the 1930s. (Walk Score 83)
